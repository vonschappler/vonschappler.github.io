Informative note for Apr/2021

I know a promised try to update this more frequently, but...

Hello everyone!
Yeah, this note title says everything - three months of silence and the failed promise to update those notes more frequently. Well, that happened for a reason: things around here were quite busy - far way more than I could expect. At least this "radio silence" was for a good reason!
After many things that happened around here, I came to some decisions which I plan to detail on this note. Most importantly is those decisions may affect me in both good and bad ways, but I really have/had to give it a try, instead of only get stuck on the "What if..." thoughts.
Let's begin with the fact that my mental health was getting even worse than before, especially, after my old PC "was done for". That made me take a quick action so I really could feel myself - at least - stable again (mentally speaking). This was the trigger for me to act "desperately", because I really saw myself in a worse spot than I already was. The main action was the start a set of chain-reactions which I will detail on this note.

Setting things together (pt. #1)


I mentioned on our last note about a number of goodies I got from my awesome community members - one of those things were the PC parts, which I decided not to put together after analyzing technical aspects of the pieces sent. Well, when my PC broke to the point of no possible fix, I had to do something or I would go deeper into a depressive state. I saw myself in a place the only way to avoid it was to get those parts and find a way to "work around" with them.
Again, I have total conscience those parts are not able to give me support to my rhythm, but it is what I have here now - so why not put them to work? I got some pieces here, other pieces there and made a "basic" PC rig - so now I have again a computer here with me.
The computer I have here now does not support me in the ways my old PC would, but look at this! I managed to get back to streaming! Not only that but also there are some new things I will share later in this note. The first stress test for the computer (streamwise) was on Feb/03/2021. I ran a few more stress tests with various games before then deciding I could stream back again!
I had the help of some people during those stress test streams, so they could help me with accurate feedback on how things were going from the "viewer" end. I have to admit I was (and still am) in a mix of joy and excitement - although I knew I would still not able to keep doing what I did before, the way I did before. But in the end, things are happening, in baby-steps, but happening. Hope is now, again, part of my life. The hope I will be able to get everything as I always wanted and make streaming a full-time job, so I will not anymore depend on the things I was used to working with before.
I can't forget to mention that a few days after I got back to streaming, my Husky Series headset got damaged. As a consequence, I tried to set up a Corsair series headset which was part of the PC parts I got. Truth be told, the headset didn't work properly. It was capturing all sounds played in my ears by the microphone and that forced me to stream with a mobile phone mic/earbud set since then. After doing some research about this issue, I discovered it was really something caused by the headset itself, and replacing it would not fix it - too many other users complaining about the same thing.
Right now, it's been more than 15 days now I am streaming like this. So if you wish (and can) help me to get something better than this mic/earbud stuff for streaming, considering make a donation here. This won't really be something just for me, but for you as well, as a community member of my stream channel, because it will help in stream quality improvements - that's for sure!

Setting things together (pt. #2)


Even with all the pros and cons mentioned before, I NEED to keep trying. And here I am, trying something new. It seems that finally, I will be able to give life to my YouTube Channel. I probably (at this point), have taken a bigger step than I should, risking falling down if I miss following steps I need to walk in this path, but again, I had to stop being stuck on the "what if..." thoughts.
Even aware of my financial condition, and after having serious conversations with some people, I decided to acquire a new game for my collection. This is one of the few times I buy games in those "pre-sale" offers we have around there, but IT WAS WORTH the try, I must say. As my psychologist mentioned lots of times to me: "It's always worth to try and learn with the mistakes then get inert and later being tormented by the 'I should have done this when I could...' thought.
I can name the few games I bought in situations like this:
  • Cyberpunk 2077
  • Destiny 2 (Shadowkeep and Beyond Light expansions)
  • Guild Wars 2
  • Vampire Bloodlines 2
but now, this list has another title: Outriders. Yes, you read it right! I bought "Outriders", which will be released on Apr/01/2021, and this will be the entrance for me to get my YouTube Channel active.
Before getting my hands on the full game, I played the demo of the game and then took the decision to use that as a possible source to create content for the channel. The first real content I created was a review video (this video has - as well as all other Outriders video will have - main audio in PT-BR, with subs in PT-BR for the deafen and EN-US for the non-Portuguese speakers) and my plan is to focus the most possible in creating content for this game!
At this moment, for doing it all by myself (recording, editing, writing subtitles, etc.), I still can't be sure or create a schedule for when to post this kind of content, so in between, I will add some "past stream" videos there, to keep the channel up and running in between each Outriders video upload.
Needless to say, since working on everything by myself, it will imply some important changes to my usual schedule, as I will detail in the next session of this note. I also can't forget about other things I do for this community - which are also done all by myself. Things like setting new discord bots, creating interactivity tools for my streams, updating this website are a few of those things I do, and they all demand the same attention as maintaining a YouTube Channel. I expect already things will get heavy on my shoulders - at least until I get used to all of these changes -, but I will be strong enough to carry on, and with your constant support, this will be a pleasure to work on all of this!
