Informative note for Sep/2018

Some important information for you

I understand the need to post something like this, especially after so much time inactive, as well as presenting some justifications for some choices made for channel evolution. This note will be made in the form of a Q&A, to facilitate how I present my ideas and justifications.
That way I ask for the understanding of all of you and thank you for your patience in reading this document which is very important.

Why so long absent?


Right. Let's start evaluating things as follows. Outside the "show business", I also have a private life, which in recent times has been very troubled. Some disappointments with people I trusted, with the work do outside the gamer world, among other things, constant problems with my equipment, problems with internet provider (incidentally, problems that have not been solved until now - 3 months later of several complaints and calls for technical assistance). These disappointments were directly influencing my physical and mental health, to the point that I really saw the need to pause.

What was I doing during this time?


In this period of (forced) pause, although I was not directly producing content, I kept working on a few things to improve the quality of what I produce, bringing something more and more professional and referring to a specific brand. I want to remember that I was already planning to pause to do all of this, but this pause would be well planned, so I had something pre-produced to maintain the life of the channel. Unfortunately, the conditions that were imposed on me by adverse situations did not allow this.
Well, things happen as they have to be and even annoyed with all these problems, I dedicated my time to a total redesign of the channel and the social networks associated with it - this includes this website.

Some of these changes comes with the intention of bringing more variety of content besides following the basic premise of my way of playing: "Play for fun"... When I had problems with equipment and lost several videos recorded, prepared to do editions and/or to ready to de puloaded, this incident really left me very disheartened to continue the series The Division and Mass Effect.
Something similar happened to me with the Dragon Ages series (which I was doing a series on live streams). Origin application for some reason that E.A. can not explain or solve, has been causing major problems, preventing the execution of any E.A. games linked to it. The only way to solve the problem (and I say beforehand - it is not a definitive solution, since this problem repeats itself), is to uninstall the Origin application together with all the games and reinstall everything again. The big consequence of this: games which do not allow cloud saves lose all the progress and everything needs to be restarted.
The combination of these factors justifies the reason why I have totally changed the channel's agenda. This new agenda, however, I will inform you, is not fixed or definitive, changes may occur as my desire and if necessary.

Why using three different streaming platforms?


The answer to that question is not as simple as it sounds.
Before posting a true opinion, I am still evaluating all the possibilities, opinions and suggestions given by people that I consider. While I am in the process of evaluating, looking for new partners to grow together, play together and offer mutual help, this is how I plan to organize my content on these platforms will be as follows:
  • YouTube: The YouTube channel will ONLY be used for pre-recorded gameplay videos, vLogs/VoD storage, compilations from my live clips or series of finished games.
  • Twitch: The Twitch channel will be my main channel for streams. All streams scheduled will be made LIVE directly to Twitch.
  • Mixer: It's an experimental channel. Twitch streams will be replayed in real time in Mixer allowing people who have Mixer accounts to interact with me during my lives. This also allows and gives the opportunity for multiplatform players to join my gameplays in lives, when that opportunity appears, since for access to the Mixer, an XLive account is required.
