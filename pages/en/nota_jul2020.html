SSL and my Website changes


Since I brought up this website, it's being hosted in a free hosting service and using a free domain. This is not a definitive step and won't be, since plans to buy and order pay hosting services are being planned.
Well, using a free hosting service with a free domain, have its advantages and its disadvantages. Not being possible to register an SSL certificate to it is one of these disadvantages and because of that I was forced to change a few things to reflect the MDN policies embedding contents.
As some of you may have noticed, it was possible to watch my streams (from my partners and mine) directly from this website. Well, according to the new policies, the only way to make that possible IS by having an SSL certificate registered to my domain and, as mentioned, those certificates are not provided (well, sold - expensively) to free domains.
In order to fix it, I had to make some changes to my site code. Since I was taking this time to make those changes, I also changed the layout of some other parts of the site, as listed below:
  • Moved all videos used on my website code to my YouTube channel
    • This change may cause some of the videos to present muted parts of it, due to the DMCA policies which are applied on YouTube (and Twitch - the reason why the document from the link is related to Twitch and my streams).

  • Changed all the code where those embed videos were present to make the calls from my YouTube channel

  • Removed all embeds that were pointing to Twitch, including the option to watch streams directly from my website
    • This means that the new Partners page layout has now only the option to follow their websites and that on my landing page there is no way to watch my streams. Only a "Visit Twitch" button is present now on those pages, allowing you to visit the page and follow them if you want.

  • Added a Schedule link to the navigation bar
    • Having my schedule where it was placed on the website, made the access to it "hidden" to those who didn't have interest in navigating the whole site to look for my stream schedule.

  • Moved the game reviews from News page to the Gaming page
    • New reviews are going to be posted there as well when they are ready to be posted.
I honestly consider those changes a great step for both the structure of the website and to do my best to follow the SSL policies required for a website to be online.
