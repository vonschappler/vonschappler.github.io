This is (sadly for us, as a community) something WE have to deal with until I can invest money on the stream itself.
Talking about my ISP, all I can detail, in advance, is that my current (and only possible ISP choice) is being a pain. After a few calls to the tech support of my ISP, this is what I could get as information from them:
  • There is a server to control DNS renews from users. This server is programmed to reset all connections older than 15 days as part of self-maintenance of the system.
  • This connection reset USUALLY happens - for my luck - during the time I have free to stream, and usually on Sundays.
  • With this reset, all in and out connections directly from my CPU to Twitch and other online services get off for the duration of the system reset.
The big problem on all this "maintenance" is simple: on our last 3 stream sessions, this "maintenance" happened more than it should happen. Each of the previous sessions I got disconnect three times and that is now being a big problem. I contacted again the tech support of my ISP and they said it was necessary to update to my router (which they said should be completed TODAY - considering the date I am writing and posting this) and asked me to make tests. All WE can hope is that this is fixed. Aside from that, nothing else I can do.
